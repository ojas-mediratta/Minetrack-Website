Introduction & Background

Estimating motion from visual input is a central problem in robotics and computer vision. Visual Odometry (VO) recovers 3D poses from a sequence of camera frames. Reliable VO is critical for successful robot autonomy.

Traditional VO pipelines rely on feature detection and geometric optimization, and are sensitive to visual repetition and lighting changes . More recent reviews emphasize learning methods more robust to scale and lighting shifts , .

We propose MineTrack, a learning-based visual odometry framework implemented for Minecraft. Minecraft provides a procedurally-generated 3D world, enabling reproducible experimentation without significant noise. Using a custom mod, we log ground-trugh Minecraft frames and transforms. Our dataset consists of recorded gameplay and trajectories at fixed resolution.

MineTrack adopts a modular approach: unsupervised models (autoencoders) learn compact visual embeddings, passed to a supervised network that predicts relative motion between frames. By decoupling representation learning from pose regression, we evaluate whether unsupervised embeddings capture sufficient geometric structure for accurate trajectory reconstruction. Predicted transformations are composed and compared to ground truth to measure drift and trajectory error.

Problem Definition

Reliable motion estimation from visual input is a core requirement for autonomous navigation.
Visual Odometry estimates trajectory by estimating consecutive transforms between camera poses.
MineTrack tackles learning-based visual odometry in a controlled 3D environment.
Given two consecutive RGB frames from Minecraft, we aim to predict the relative motion of the agent between frames (translation and rotation).
By composing predicted frame-to-frame transforms over time, we reconstruct the full trajectory and measure drift versus ground truth.

Research Question

Do self-supervised visual embeddings learn geometry features that improve downstream relative pose regression and reduce trajectory drift?

Objectives

  • Learn compact visual embeddings from played minecraft frames
  • Predict frame to frame motion and translate to trajectories
  • Evaluate trajectory accuracy and drift vs ground truth across varied environments

Scope

Monocular RGB visual odometry in Minecraft with frame-to-frame pose regression and trajectory reconstruction via transform composition.

Motivation

Minecraft provides easier data collection and a more constrained environment than the real world, allowing for easier model development and testing.
Models validated in Minecraft can be transferred to photorealistic simulators with more realistic physics engines and sensor modules, with the ultimate goal being SLAM in real-world environments.

Methods

Approach Overview

We solve VO as a regression of image-to-image transforms between RGB input images. We propose a two-stage encoder-regressor model to produce vector embeddings from input images and transform outputs from embeddings.

Machine Learning Algorithms

CNN encoders are well-studied and effective for odometry . The first stage is a CNN encoder trained as an autoencoder with self-supervision. The second stage uses an MLP to take two embedding vectors from each sequential pair of images and estimate a 3D transform between them, or an RNN ingesting a series of embedding vectors and using its own state to produce a series of output transforms. Vision transformers (ViTs) are also a strong candidate due to their sequential image handling . Therefore, we also propose a ViT as an alternative to the encoder-regressor pipeline, using image patch embedding and a transformer encoder with pose regression head for output.

Evaluation Strategy

We propose the following Visual Odometry metrics to compare the performance of our various models: Absolute Trajectory Error (ATE) which measures absolute RMS error across the whole trajectory , Relative Pose Error (RPE) which measures local RMS transform accuracy, , and Translation/Rotation error using the KITTI standard .

Potential Dataset

Data Sources

Our dataset will come from our custom Minecraft mod, which captures frames and associated pose data from standard Minecraft gameplay. With a few hours of gameplay per team member we will have access to a dataset with millions of labelled images. To ensure adaptability across different biomes in Minecraft worlds we will target a minimum of 20 minutes of unique gameplay per biome.

Data Preprocessing Plan

We will use the data collection mod to crop and scale the original rendered Minecraft frames to 256x256 square. Additionally, we will experiment with data augmentation by modulation of scene brightenss and contrast and introduction of random noise to decrease the brittleness of the vision encoder.

Potential Results & Discussion

Expected Outcomes

We anticipate our model will accurately and robustly track player motion in Minecraft from rendered video with moderate resource usage.

Anticipated Performance Metrics

Based on the benchmarks for learning-based methods on the KITTI Dataset from , we anticipate achieving a relative translation error and rotation error ≤ 10% and ≤ 10° respectively for roughly KITTI-length visual odometry sequences.

Potential Impact

Algorithms developed in MineTrack could be adapted to track robot motion in the real world with less energy and compute use than current-gen models, reducing resource usage.
